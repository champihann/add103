About Me

Personal Background:

Hello! My name is Shawnna Hann. I grew up in Ann Arbor, Michigan, as the youngest of nine siblings: Emily, Cyndi, Genny, Hannah, Laura, Chrissy, David, and Tamayra. My childhood was a little wild and filled with fun, shaped by the lively dynamics of a large family. Some of our most cherished family traditions include making delicious apple pudding and pizza casserole together.

From a young age, sports played a significant role in my life; I have been playing softball, basketball, and bowling for as long as I can remember. Among my family and friends, I often talk about my dog, Cooper, who is truly the best dog in the whole world. His funny quirks and personality bring so much joy and laughter to our lives.

Professional/Academic Goals:

My love for technology is what initially inspired me to pursue a career in Software Application Design & Development. This semester, I'm still exploring my specific interests and career goals, but I'm confident that, after a few more classes at McHenry County College, I'll have a clearer sense of direction.

My long-term aspiration is to develop the technical skills needed to work as a QA specialist. Throughout my career, I’ve gained valuable experience in various professional environments, and now I’m eager to focus on a specific area within software development. I am committed to growing and learning every day, and I hope to connect with fellow students and professionals through MCC’s program. If you'd like to connect professionally, feel free to reach out to me on LinkedIn!

Personality/Strengths:

I would describe my personality as ISTP (Introverted, Sensing, Thinking, Perceiving), based on the Myers-Briggs personality test. I'm not the biggest fan of talking about myself, but if you get to know me, you'll find I have a dry, deadpan sense of humor.

One of my greatest strengths is my visual-spatial intelligence, which comes in handy both in my studies and my hobbies. I’m also pretty good at sports, something that has been a big part of my life. At the end of the day, I embrace who I am—unique and true to myself. I am who I am, and no one can change that, not even me.
